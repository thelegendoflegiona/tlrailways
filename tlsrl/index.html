Biomes

Seven distinct biomes crossed along the 3,066-block measured route. Biome transitions are a primary driver of the route's directionality changes — particularly through Phase 1.

7 BIOMES TRAVERSED
01
The Complex Terrain Zone

Segments S6–S18 (13 segments, 535 blocks) contain 7 of the 13 turns in just 17% of the route distance. This tight cluster at ~X -493 to -773 is where the majority of bridges and tunnels likely concentrate — the route threading through the most technically challenging terrain on the line.

02
The Northward Corrections at Valley Approach

S25 and S27 are the only two northward segments on the route. Located just before The LoL Valley Station, the track dips to Z=1314 then corrects back to Z=1286. This manoeuvre suggests a terrain obstacle — likely a ridge or cliff — requiring the alignment to find a lower crossing point before ascending to station level.

03
Long Corridor Efficiency

The two longest segments (S5 at 466 blocks W and S19 at 456 blocks S) account for 30% of the total route distance in just 2 of 29 segments. These long, uninterrupted runs indicate that the terrain in these corridors was flat enough — or already cleared — to allow straight-line construction.

04
Route vs. Straight Line

Direct distance from start to Valley Station is ≈ 2,207 blocks. The actual route is 3,066 blocks — 38.9% longer than the straight-line path. For a railway navigating 7 biomes, 2 tunnels, and 11 bridges, this is a respectable efficiency ratio.
